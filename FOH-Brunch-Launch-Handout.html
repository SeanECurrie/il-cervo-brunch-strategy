Why This Brunch Is Different

This isn't "we added brunch." This is the launch of something nobody else in Milwaukee is doing. We researched every brunch restaurant in the city — there is no Italian brunch in Milwaukee. Not one. We're first.

"Italian brunch, 9th floor, from a Food & Wine Best New Chef."
— That's our position. Nobody else can say it.

About Chef Mike

Chef Mike Sheerin is not just a new hire. He is a 2010 Food & Wine Best New Chef — one of the most prestigious awards in American dining. He trained at:

  • Blackbird (Chicago) — Michelin-starred. Mike was Paul Kahan's first-ever chef de cuisine.
  • Jean-Georges (NYC) — three Michelin stars
  • WD-50 (NYC) — Wylie Dufresne's legendary molecular gastronomy restaurant
  • Trenchermen, Embeya, Cicchetti — Chicago restaurants he opened and led

When a guest asks about the chef, you have a real story to tell. Not "he's great" — "he's a Food & Wine Best New Chef who ran kitchens at Michelin-starred restaurants in New York and Chicago." That sentence changes how people experience the meal.

The Menu — Know These Cold

This is Chef Mike's menu — elevated, chef-driven, Italian DNA throughout (focaccia in everything, giardiniera, Calabrian aioli, San Marzano). When a guest asks "what should I get?" — lead with the showstoppers.

The Questions You'll Get Asked (and the Answers)

🅿️ #1 Question: "Where do I park?"
Sunday street parking in Milwaukee is FREE. No meters. No time limits. Every Sunday. There's street parking on Juneau Ave and surrounding blocks. The hotel also has a garage with a $10 brunch validation. Lead with free street parking.
"Where is the restaurant?"
Take the elevator to the 9th floor. Turn right when you exit. We're at the top of the Trade Hotel — 420 West Juneau, right across from Fiserv Forum. The view is worth the ride up.
"Do I need a reservation?"
Reservations are recommended and available on OpenTable. We do accept walk-ins based on availability, but Sundays can fill up. Fun fact: most brunch spots in Milwaukee don't take reservations at all — we do, so you never have to wait in a 60-minute line.
"What kind of brunch is this?"
It's an Italian brunch — the only one in Milwaukee. Think bomboloni, eggs in purgatory, mortadella Benedict, and Spritz Towers. We also have classic brunch favorites done with an Italian touch. Chef Mike Sheerin brings a Food & Wine Best New Chef pedigree to everything on the menu.
"Is this the same restaurant as dinner?"
Same space, same kitchen, same chef — but a completely different menu designed specifically for brunch. The view is actually better in the morning light.
"Who's the chef?"
Chef Mike Sheerin — he was named a Food & Wine Best New Chef in 2010 and cooked at Michelin-starred restaurants in New York and Chicago before coming to Milwaukee. He built this brunch menu from the ground up.
"What about the drag brunch?"
Drag brunch is still happening once a month — it's one of our most popular events. This is our new WEEKLY Sunday brunch — same great food, beautiful space, and the best view in Milwaukee, every single Sunday.
"Is there a kids menu?"
[Check with Mike — if yes, mention it. If no:] We don't have a formal kids menu, but we can do simple pasta, a plain pancake, or scrambled eggs for younger guests. Just ask your server and the kitchen will take care of it.

Your Job on Launch Day (and Every Sunday)

The Experience We're Creating

This isn't fast-casual brunch. This is an occasion. Guests are on the 9th floor with panoramic views of downtown Milwaukee. They're eating food from an award-winning chef. They're drinking Spritz Towers. This should feel like a special Sunday — relaxed but elevated. The energy of the room should say: "This is where you want to be on a Sunday morning."

Do This

  • Mention the view — walk guests to the window if the table isn't next to it
  • Recommend the Gold French Toast or Truffle Omelet to every table
  • Suggest the BEC Pizza for groups of 3+ ("great for sharing")
  • Mention dry aging and house-made focaccia — these signal serious kitchen
  • If they love it, mention the review card: "We'd love a Google review if you have a sec"
  • Point out the email signup at the host stand on the way out

Don't Do This

  • Don't just say "it's brunch" — say "Chef Mike's brunch" or "9th floor brunch"
  • Don't skip the chef story — Food & Wine Best New Chef is a real differentiator, not bragging
  • Don't let a table leave without hearing about the Gold French Toast, truffle omelet, or caviar add-on
  • Don't say "I think parking is $10" — say "Sunday street parking is free, and the hotel has a $10 option too"
  • Don't forget: every phone photo a guest takes is free marketing for us
