Research Methodology & Process

Il Cervo Brunch Strategy · Prepared for Chef Mike Sheerin · March 2026

This document explains exactly how the research behind the Il Cervo Brunch Market Research report was conducted — what tools we used, what sources we tapped, how we validated findings, and what level of confidence you should have in each conclusion. This isn't a black box. Every finding has a trail.

Research Architecture

We used a four-domain parallel research approach, with each domain investigated independently before cross-referencing findings. This prevents one domain's assumptions from contaminating another's conclusions.

DomainWhat We InvestigatedWhy It Matters
A. Positioning GapMilwaukee's brunch competitive landscape — who's doing what, where the gaps areYou can't position without knowing the field
B. Friction & BehaviorWhat prevents people from trying new brunch spots, hotel dining barriers, parkingGreat positioning fails if people can't or won't get there
C. Opportunity LeversYour assets — Sheerin's credentials, Italian brunch concept, rooftop, Deer District, drag brunchKnowing what you have determines what to lead with
D. Marketing LandscapeHow Milwaukee restaurants market brunch, what works, what doesn't, media channelsYour marketing team needs direction, not just a budget

After all four domains completed independently, we ran a cross-domain synthesis — looking for patterns that only become visible when you combine the findings.

Research Execution by Domain

Domain A: Positioning Gap — 14 searches, 5 scrapes

What we searched:

  • "best brunch Milwaukee 2025 2026" + variations
  • "Milwaukee brunch Italian" (testing if anyone does it)
  • "Milwaukee rooftop brunch" (testing the view angle)
  • "Milwaukee brunch downtown vs third ward vs east side"
  • "Milwaukee drag brunch" (competitive themed landscape)

What we found: 15 brunch competitors profiled. Zero Italian brunch in Milwaukee. Zero rooftop brunch above 3 floors.

Domain B: Friction & Behavior — 26 searches, 8 scrapes

  • "Trade Hotel Milwaukee dining reviews" + Il Cervo reviews
  • "hotel restaurant brunch challenges" + success factors
  • "Milwaukee downtown parking brunch" + Sunday parking rules
  • "brunch habits post COVID" (UCLA/Clemson, 34,000 respondents)
  • "Deer District Milwaukee foot traffic non-game day"

What we found: A 6-layer friction stack. Sunday street parking is FREE in Milwaukee. Google hours show "4PM-12AM" — invisible to brunch searchers.

Domain C: Opportunity Levers — 13 searches, 4 scrapes

  • "Mike Sheerin chef Chicago" + awards + press
  • "Italian brunch menu trends 2025 2026"
  • "Deer District Milwaukee development" + new venues
  • "drag brunch to regular brunch conversion"
  • "Chicago chef Milwaukee reception"

What we found: Sheerin is a Food & Wine Best New Chef (2010), Chicago Magazine Chef of the Year, trained at WD-50, Jean-Georges, Blackbird. Zero press connecting him to Il Cervo.

Domain D: Marketing Landscape — 23 searches, 6 scrapes

  • Milwaukee food influencers/media (OnMilwaukee, Journal Sentinel, MKEeeeEats)
  • "restaurant brunch marketing strategies that work 2025"
  • "restaurant marketing ROI social media vs events vs influencers"
  • "how to brief a restaurant marketing team"
  • "email marketing restaurant ROI"

What we found: Instagram-dominated via micro-influencers. Email marketing ($42 ROI per $1) is wide open — competitors don't do it. 7 failure modes documented.

Source Quality Assessment

High Confidence

Multiple independent sources, primary data:

  • Il Cervo is the only Italian brunch in Milwaukee (verified against all 15 competitors)
  • Sunday street parking is free (verified against city parking website)
  • Sheerin's credentials — Food & Wine, Blackbird, Jean-Georges (multiple press sources)
  • Google hours show 4PM-12AM (verified by direct observation)
  • Blue's Egg has 60+ minute waits (verified across multiple review sources)
Moderate Confidence

Limited sources or indirect evidence:

  • Post-COVID brunch consolidation (Mike's account + national trend data, no Milwaukee-specific study)
  • Email marketing as open channel (national ROI data, no Milwaukee-specific study)
  • Deer District development timeline (press releases — completion dates may shift)
Data Gaps — Need Mike's Input
  • Current brunch covers/revenue (no internal data)
  • Drag brunch to regular brunch conversion potential
  • Hotel guest vs. local ratio
  • Marketing team capabilities and current activities

What This Research Did NOT Cover

This was a landscape and positioning research phase — deliberately scoped to understand the market before making strategic recommendations.

  • Define a target demographic (needs Mike's operational input)
  • Design marketing campaigns (premature without positioning decisions)
  • Create a brunch menu analysis (Mike's expertise, not ours)
  • Project revenue or ROI (needs internal financial data)
  • Evaluate the marketing team's current work (need to see it first)

These are next-phase activities informed by this research + Mike's feedback.
