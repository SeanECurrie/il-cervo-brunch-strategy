What This Means for Marketing

Lead with what nobody has. When the marketing team creates content, when influencers come in, when press writes about you — the story isn't "we have brunch." The story is: "We have a $47 truffle-and-caviar omelet, gold-dusted French toast, and dry aged meatball subs on house focaccia. On the 9th floor. From a Food & Wine Best New Chef. Nobody else in Milwaukee has ANY of this."

The common items (Caesar, beet salad, French toast base concept, burger base concept) are fine to have — they give guests familiar entry points. But they don't differentiate. Don't lead marketing with the Caesar salad. Lead with the gold.

The items with 1-3 competitors (pizza, steak & eggs, salmon, burger) are in the middle — not unique but not crowded either. These differentiate through EXECUTION: dry aged beef vs. regular, focaccia vs. brioche, Calabrian aioli vs. regular aioli, hanger steak vs. flat iron. The FOH story is what separates these from the competition.
