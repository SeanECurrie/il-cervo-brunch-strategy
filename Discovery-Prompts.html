Discovery Prompts for Mike

Post-Research Conversation Guide · Il Cervo Brunch Strategy

How to use this: Not a questionnaire. These are conversation starters organized by theme. Pick the ones that feel most relevant based on what Mike reacts to in the research. Click any prompt to see why it matters. Some will trigger 30-second answers, others might unlock 10 minutes of insight.

1 Validating What We Found

Check our research against Mike's lived experience.

On the competitive landscape

+ why
We identified 15 brunch competitors. Did we miss anyone? Are there spots that locals consider top-tier that didn't show up?
Locals know things Google doesn't. A place that doesn't rank well online might dominate word-of-mouth. Missing a key competitor would change our positioning analysis.
+ why
We found zero Italian brunch in Milwaukee. Does that match your read? Has anyone tried it and failed?
If someone tried Italian brunch and it failed, we need to know why. If it's never been attempted, that's a wide-open opportunity — but we need to confirm it's not because there's no demand.
+ why
Blue's Egg, Uncle Wolfie's, Cafe Benelux came up as dominant. Do you see them as your competition, or is Il Cervo going after a different crowd?
If Mike sees himself competing with these spots, the strategy is "steal share." If he's after a different crowd entirely, it's "create a new category." Different playbooks.

On the friction points

+ why
Your Google hours still show 4PM–12AM. Has brunch been added?
If this hasn't been fixed, it's the single highest-priority action. Every dollar spent on marketing is wasted if someone Googles "Il Cervo brunch" and sees "opens at 4PM."
+ why
Sunday street parking is free in Milwaukee — no meters, no time limits. Did you know? Is the $10 pass for the hotel garage specifically?
If guests can park for free on the street and you're advertising $10 parking, you're creating friction that doesn't need to exist. This could be reframed as "free street parking available" which removes a barrier entirely.
+ why
How do people currently find out about Il Cervo? What's the most common first-visit path?
Understanding the current discovery funnel tells us what's working (double down) and what's missing (fix). If it's mostly hotel guests, the local marketing strategy is different than if locals already know about it.

On the opportunity levers

+ why
We found your credentials (Food & Wine Best New Chef, Blackbird, Jean-Georges, WD-50) but zero press connecting you to Il Cervo. Is that intentional?
This is the biggest gap between what Il Cervo HAS and what Milwaukee KNOWS. A single profile piece in OnMilwaukee or the Journal Sentinel could change awareness overnight. If there's a reason it hasn't happened, we need to know.
+ why
The Deer District development — new concert venue, second hotel, public museum. How much of this do you see day-to-day?
Press releases say the district is booming. But if foot traffic is still dead on non-game days, the development is future potential, not current reality. This changes the timeline for the strategy.

2 Filling the Data Gaps

Things we couldn't research from the outside. Mike's answers sharpen every recommendation.

Internal numbers

+ why
How many covers on drag brunch Sundays? What's the average check?
This is the baseline for Sunday demand. If drag brunch fills 120 seats at $60/head, we know the ceiling. Regular brunch needs to hit a percentage of that to be viable.
+ why
What percentage of dinner business is hotel guests vs. locals?
If dinner is 80% hotel guests, brunch marketing needs to acquire an entirely new audience. If locals already come for dinner, brunch marketing is "come back Sunday morning" — much easier.
+ why
Any data from the first brunch attempt 2.5 years ago? What was tried, what happened, why did it stop?
History is intelligence. If brunch failed because of the parking, that's different from failing because of awareness, menu, or staffing. The old failure mode tells us what to solve differently.

The marketing team

+ why
Who are they? Agency, freelancer, in-house? What's their background?
An Instagram-native freelancer needs different direction than a full-service agency. The brief we build depends on their capabilities.
+ why
Do they have a brief, or were they just told "promote the restaurant"?
If there's no brief, our Marketing Team Brief section in the main report becomes their Day 1 document. If they have a brief, we need to see it and compare against our research.

Drag brunch specifics

+ why
Do you capture email addresses from drag brunch ticket buyers?
If yes: you have a warm list of people who already come to Il Cervo on Sundays. Email them about regular brunch — cheapest acquisition channel possible ($42 ROI per $1). If no: start immediately.
+ why
Have any drag brunch attendees come back for dinner? Any sense of conversion?
If drag brunch attendees already convert to dinner, they'll convert to regular brunch. If they don't, drag brunch might be attracting an audience that loves the show but not the restaurant — different strategy needed.

3 Strategic Forks

Decisions Mike needs to make. Our research informs them, but they require his judgment.

Positioning

+ why
Do you want to own "Italian brunch" as a category, or is this "brunch that happens to be at an Italian restaurant"?
These are different positioning strategies. "Italian brunch" means bomboloni, Spritz Towers, and eggs in purgatory are the LEAD. "Brunch at an Italian place" means you lead with brunch classics and the Italian stuff is a bonus. The research says the Italian angle is the differentiator — but Mike has to want it.
+ why
"Italian brunch, 9th floor, Food & Wine Best New Chef" — does that tagline resonate, or does it feel like it's trying too hard?
If it resonates, that's the positioning statement for every piece of marketing. If it feels forced, we need to find the version that's authentically Mike.
+ why
The $45 Boozy Brunch — is that where you want to be? Milwaukee brunch ranges from $12–18 (casual) to $30–50 (upscale).
$45 positions you at the top of the market. That's a legitimate choice — but it means the experience has to justify the price every single Sunday. The view and the cocktails help, but we need to know if that's intentional positioning or inherited pricing.

Scale and timing

+ why
What would success look like on Sundays that would trigger adding Saturday?
Having a specific trigger (e.g., "4 consecutive Sundays above 80 covers") prevents both premature expansion and missed opportunity. We want to help set that threshold based on the data.
+ why
What's the staffing situation? Pulling dinner staff or hiring brunch-specific?
Staffing affects consistency. If it's the same team doing both, burnout and quality drops are risks. If it's dedicated brunch staff, there's a cost floor that needs to be covered by brunch revenue.

The Chicago question

+ why
Are you "a Chicago chef bringing something new" or "your new Milwaukee chef who happens to have cooked in Chicago"?
Milwaukee has its own culinary confidence now. Dan Jacobs (also Chicago-to-Milwaukee) proved the path: lead with national credentials (Top Chef), not geographic origin. Our recommendation: lead with "Food & Wine Best New Chef" not "from Chicago." But it's Mike's story to tell.
+ why
Have you connected with Milwaukee food media yet? OnMilwaukee, Journal Sentinel dining, MKEeeeEats?
A single profile piece could change the awareness game overnight. If he hasn't connected yet, that's Action #1.
